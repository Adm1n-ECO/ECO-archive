# ECO Tribal Outreach Strategy

---

## WHY THIS MARKET

Five conditions that make institutional tribal sales work:
1. IGRA mandates cultural budget — not discretionary spending
2. Defined community with formal membership
3. Cultural preservation urgency — language and tradition under active threat
4. Generational gap — elders hold knowledge, youth are disconnected
5. Institutional buyer — one invoice, one approval, one relationship

**Budget reality:** $3,600/year for 200 seats is 0.36% of Thunder Valley's
$1M/year local nonprofit giving. The dollar amount is NEVER the obstacle.
Questions tribal decision-makers actually ask:
- Is our data sovereign?
- Does it serve our cultural preservation mission?
- Will our members use it — especially the young ones?
- Can we customize it for our specific language?
- Who else is using it?

**Contact rule:** Cultural affairs director or language program lead — NOT casino marketing or IT.
Cultural budget conversations move faster than technology budgets.

## TRIBAL REFERRAL DYNAMIC

Tribal communities communicate through intertribal organizations, gaming compact
networks, and shared advocacy groups.

**Strategy:** Establish ONE California success first. Document it. Let that tribe
become the reference. Then expand. Do not pitch multiple tribes simultaneously.

One tribal success in Indian Country Today or Tribal Business News is worth
more than any paid marketing campaign.
