The quality guarantee
"Every profile on EternalCurrent.Online is a real person, created by someone who loves them."

Because creating a profile requires a small payment, our users only build profiles for people who genuinely matter to them. There are no ghost profiles, no placeholder entries, no junk data. When you search for a family member on EternalCurrent.Online, every result you see is a real human being — vetted by the person who loves them most.

Decisions still needed — pricing specifics
Open decision · Who pays for invited profiles?
When you invite your brother in London to connect — does he pay $1.99 to create his own profile, or do you pay to add him to your tree?
AInvitee pays — each person pays $1.99 to create their own profile when they accept.
BInviter pays — you pay $1.99 to add someone, they join free.
CEach household only pays for profiles they personally create.
Open decision · Managed profiles (elderly, non-device users)
A user wants to add their 82-year-old grandmother who will never have a phone. Can they create and own her profile for $1.99, or does every profile need its own login?
AManaged profiles allowed — child creates and owns the profile, no login required for subject.
BEvery profile requires its own email and login.
CManaged allowed but limited — no ability to accept connections independently.
Open decision · Deleted profiles
A user paid $1.99 to create a profile and now wants to delete it. What happens?
ANon-refundable — profile deleted, payment kept (like a seat purchase).
BSoft delete — 30 days to restore, then permanent. No refund.
CArchive — data preserved but hidden. Restore for free any time.
DCredit returned to account balance for use on a new profile.
Discuss — payment timing in the signup flow In the value-first signup flow, payment is triggered when a user tries to invite their first connection. At that moment they pay for their own profile plus any household members they want to add. The question is: do they pay for everyone upfront at that moment, or add profiles one at a time and pay as they go?
