How It Works

A platform built around you,
not someone else's root.

EternalCurrent is a personal network visualization platform. You are the center. Every feature is designed to hold the complexity of a real diaspora family — without flattening it, translating it, or handing it to someone else.

01
Getting started
You create your network. You name it.

Choose a name from a curated list — Lightning, Ember, Tide, Meridian, Solstice — or propose your own. This is your family's name for your network. Not an account. Not a profile. A living map with a name that belongs to you.

The AI asks how you want to start. There are no forms, no labeled fields, no submit buttons. Every piece of data you give the platform comes through conversation, a single tap, or a file you choose to share.
02
Your family enters the map
People join. The globe populates.

You invite family members. They accept. Each person's birth city and current city becomes a dot on the globe — connected by a migration arc colored by their cultural origin. Amber for India. Teal for Fiji. Purple for Pakistan. The geography of your family, visible for the first time on one screen.

You control who sees what. Every profile has a visibility setting — the Network Owner sees everything, family members see what you choose to share, guests see only what you invite them to see.
03
The AI starts asking questions
Not data entry. A conversation.

Once per visit, the AI surfaces one question — specific to your network, weighted toward the stories most at risk of being lost. Passed family members first. Older relatives second. Living memory before it fades. Never assuming what you know, what you observe, or what role you play in a ceremony it has never asked about.

"When Sahiba — or maybe one of her parents, or her sister, or someone else who knows her — added her to the network, her upcoming wedding sounds like a fun and exciting chance for the whole family to get together. As her mama, which activities will you have a chance to participate in?"
04
The vocabulary comes alive
The words your family actually uses.

EternalCurrent holds the kinship terms families actually use — not dictionary definitions, but the living words with their register distinctions, regional variations, and the emotional weight that translation erases. Bhayia. Jij. Nana. Mama. Your family's vocabulary, held exactly as it is.

The Discovery Banner introduces one new term or cultural tradition each visit — from your own heritage and from cultures now entering your network through marriages, adoptions, and new connections. A bridge, not a mirror.
05
The network stays yours
No auto-renewal. No data sales. No ads.

You choose to renew, every period. If you stop, your network is frozen, not deleted — preserved for twelve months in case you return. When you ask for complete removal, it is gone. Until then, it is yours, intact, exactly as you left it. Nobody else's asset. Nobody else's research data. Nobody else's product.

EternalCurrent products are ad-free. Your data is never sold, never licensed, never used for research without your explicit consent. The subscription is the revenue. The data is yours.

Manifesto

You are not a leaf
on someone else's tree.

The problem with trees

Every platform that claims to hold your family history puts someone at the top. A patriarch. A founder. A root. Everyone else is a branch, a leaf, a descendant — defined by their distance from the person someone decided should be the center.

That is not how families work. That is not how identity works. That is not how any person actually experiences the people who shaped them.

You are not a leaf on your father's tree. You are the center of your own field of energy. The people above you, beside you, and below you — all of them radiating outward from where you stand.

"Every person here has shaped who you are — whether you know their story or not."

The problem with translation

Every genealogy platform was built for families whose history fits in English. One word for uncle. One word for grandfather. No distinction between the mother's brother and the father's brother, between the older sister and the younger one, between the formal address and the intimate one.

But your family has Mama — your mother's brother, who has a specific relationship to you that "uncle" does not begin to carry. Your family has Nana — your mother's father, a word that English uses for grandmother and gets wrong every time. Your family has Bhayia — not just older brother, but a term of respect and warmth that jumped a generation and stuck, because your son grew up watching your siblings use it and understood what it meant before he understood the word.

These words are not translation problems. They are the culture itself. When they disappear — as they disappear in every diaspora family, between the first generation and the third — something irreplaceable goes with them.

"Different families use different words. There is no wrong answer — just the words your family actually uses."

The problem with data

23andMe collected the DNA of fifteen million people and put it in a bankruptcy auction. The most intimate data a human being can generate — their genetic code, their ancestry, their family connections — offered to the highest bidder.

This is not an accident. It is the inevitable conclusion of a business model that treats your data as the product. When the subscription revenue runs out, the data is what remains. And the data, as it turns out, is worth selling.

EternalCurrent was built on a different premise: your data is not our asset. It never was. The subscription is the revenue. The data is yours. If you ask for complete removal, it is gone. Until then, it is preserved exactly as you left it — yours, intact, belonging to no one else.

Not because we are generous. Because we believe it is not ours to take.

"Only Yours. Forever."

What we are building

EternalCurrent is a personal network visualization platform. It holds your family's geography as migration arcs on a globe. It holds your family's vocabulary as a living library of terms, with the nuance that translation erases. It holds the stories your elders told, in their words, with their names attached, as oral history records that will outlast anyone who was in the room.

It asks questions that no other platform is asking — about the person who crossed the border in 1947, about the grandmother who chose a word for the relationship that didn't have a name in English, about the ceremony you attended last month and what it meant to you and whether your family does it the same way or differently and whether you even know.

It does not assume you follow the traditions of your heritage. It does not assume you know what your heritage's traditions are. It asks. It listens. It holds whatever you give it, exactly as you give it.

Who it is for

It is for families who live in more than one country and more than one language simultaneously. For families whose story crosses borders that did not exist when their grandparents were born. For families who have a word for something English does not, and who are watching that word disappear in their children's mouths.

It is for the student in an Ethnic Studies class who has spent a semester learning why cultural preservation matters and wants to do something about it with their own family. For the tribal language keeper who needs a platform that treats vocabulary as sovereignty, not data. For the diaspora family that has tried every genealogy tool and found that none of them were built for them.

It is not for everyone. We do not want everyone. We want the families for whom this is the thing that was missing — the platform that finally sees them the way they actually are.

What we believe

We believe that your family's story belongs to your family. Not to a platform, not to a database, not to a pharmaceutical company, not to anyone who might one day acquire the platform and decide that the data is worth more than the promise that was made when you gave it.

We believe that the words a community uses to describe its own relationships and its own history are worth preserving exactly as they are — not translated into someone else's vocabulary, not tidied up for someone else's comfort.

We believe that you are not a leaf on a tree. You are the center of a field of energy. Every connection radiates outward from where you stand. Every arc on the globe is a decision someone made, a border someone crossed, a city someone chose to call home. Every word in the vocabulary library is a relationship that mattered enough to name.

We built this because no one else had. We are still building it. We would be grateful if you joined us.
