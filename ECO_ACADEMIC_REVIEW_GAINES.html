Is ECO using the right language?
A review for Professor Frederick Gaines

EternalCurrent.Online is building a platform for diaspora families. Before it launches, we want to know whether we are using the vocabulary of Ethnic Studies correctly — or making the kinds of errors that signal an outsider working from surface knowledge.

What this review is

ECO uses fifteen concepts drawn from Ethnic Studies, linguistics, and oral history methodology to describe what it does and why it matters. These are not kinship terms from a specific language — they are the conceptual vocabulary of the platform itself: the words we use to frame the problem we are solving and the work we are doing.

If we are using any of these terms incorrectly — too loosely, too narrowly, or in ways that would make a scholar wince — we need to know before the platform launches. A platform that misuses the vocabulary of the discipline it claims to serve would undermine its own credibility with exactly the communities it most wants to reach.

For each term: we show you how ECO currently uses or frames it, and we ask whether that usage is academically sound. You can confirm it, flag it as wrong or imprecise, or mark it as contested or variable. The notes field is where the most valuable feedback usually lives.

About you

For each term: Expand the card, read how ECO uses it, and mark it Accurate (we have it right), Imprecise or Wrong (we are misusing it or missing something important), or Contested (the term itself is debated in the discipline). The correction and notes fields are where the most useful feedback lives. You do not need to review every term — whatever you have time for.
