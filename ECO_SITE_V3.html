The exchange

The more you share,
the more you receive.
The more you give back.

Every story enriches what comes back to you. Every person who joins brings something you did not have. Nothing disappears.

01
Start with yourself

You are the center. Always.

You create your network and name it — Lightning, Ember, Tide, Meridian. You are at the center. Not the oldest person. Not the patriarch. Not whoever started the tree. Nobody else can build your network from your position. Nobody else sees it the way you do.

02
Talk. Nothing gets lost.

Stories emerge the way they always have.

Each person in your network has a structural relationship and the name your family actually uses for them. Not the dictionary word. The living word. Everything surfaces through conversation — the way knowledge has always moved between people who know each other.

03
Your Eternal, made visible

The journeys that made you, on one globe.

Migration arcs connect where everyone was born to where they are now — each colored by the cultural origin it carries. For the first time, you see your family not as a list of names but as movement. A diaspora that crossed borders and oceans and landed where it landed for reasons worth knowing.

04
Your Current arrives

What arrives before the moment passes.

Some questions have a window. The story an elder holds. The memory of a place that no longer exists. The right question surfaces at the right moment — specific, unhurried, never assuming. When you answer, it stays. For you, for your network, and for the ones who are not born yet.

05
Your Current departs

What you share becomes someone's Eternal.

The story you share right now becomes part of what a younger person carries forward. The memory you record today becomes someone else's Eternal tomorrow. You are always both — receiving and transmitting. What moves through you in this moment gets held, for everyone in your network, and the ones who come after.

Who does what

Three roles. One rule: you are always in control.

Conductor
$5.99 / month · No auto-renewal

Every member of the network. You share what you know, respond to what arrives, and control what others can see about you. The network belongs to its Conductors, not to the platform.

Keeper
No added cost · Designated by the founder

The cultural teacher of the network. Annotates profiles, authors vocabulary, creates curriculum. Title is community-defined — Elder, Storyteller, Granthi, Sensei. Whatever the network decides.

Moderator
No added cost · Nominated by peers

Handles disputed relationships and flagged content. A steward of accuracy and care — not a gatekeeper.

Data ownership

Everything shared belongs only to you.

Everything in your network came from your network. It is not ours to hold, sell, or transfer. You are not the product. You never will be.

No auto-renewal

Every renewal is a deliberate choice. We earn your continued trust every single period.

Freeze, don't delete

If you stop paying, your network waits — not disappears. The stories are still there when you come back.

No data monetization

The subscription is the model. Your stories, your vocabulary, your network — none of it generates revenue for us.

The ECO Manifesto

The story that
shaped you.
The one you are
building now.

Other platforms showed you the route. The trunk. The branch. The leaf — and the leaf was you.

They drew the structure and called it your history. But a tree without its storms is just furniture.

A tree implies one direction. Culture does not travel that way.

A word moves through a grandmother's kitchen into a child who is not even paying attention — absorbed through repetition, through the smell of the food it accompanied, through the way her hands moved when she said it. That same word moves through the neighbor who visited every Sunday. The child grows up and uses the word without knowing where it came from. They have become a conductor of something they never consciously learned.

A tradition survives because someone who married into the family fell in love with it and carried it more faithfully than anyone born to it. A language is kept alive by a teacher, not the bloodline. The bloodline let it drift. The teacher held it.

The pollinators that kept the family flowering — the neighbor who brought food during the hard year, the teacher who saw something in a child nobody else noticed, the friend who stayed when everyone else left. They are in your field even if they never appear on the branch. The ones who sat in the shade carry something of your Eternal into a world you will never see.

Here there is no tree. Only energy — Compounding, Communicating, Conjoining.

Energy that moves in all directions simultaneously. That does not require a structure to be real. That is conducted by people who share no blood with you, arrived through storms nobody documented, sustained by pollinators nobody thought to name. Your field of energy is wider than any tree could hold.

On Eternal and Current

Eternal is what it was, and what it will be. The storms your grandmother survived are in you whether or not they made a record. The story you share today will be in the record for the people who are not born yet. Both ends of time, held in the same place.

Current is this moment — how it is flowing into you from the people in your network, and how you in this moment are flowing outward into someone else's. You are always both. Receiving and transmitting simultaneously. What moves through you becomes part of the permanent record. Your Eternal, and someone else's.

On what belongs to you

Nothing that enters gets lost. The story your grandmother told. The city your grandfather left. The word your family uses that has no English equivalent. Held faithfully, exactly as it was shared, belonging only to you.

Everything in your network came from your network. Other platforms made you the product — your attention, your relationships, your family's story, mapped and monetized. You are not the product. You never will be.

On what we are building

We want the right 200,000 families. Not 200 million. Any family whose story is too complex, too multilingual, too cross-cultural for a tree to hold — and that is most families, in every culture. The Italian-Jewish family in New Jersey. The Irish-Spanish family in California. The South Asian family spread across four continents. The tribal family preserving a language with no written form. The family where the grandparents speak one language, the parents another, and the children a third. The tree model was built for one kind of family. This was built for everyone the tree left out.

The story you tell today will be here for the people who are not born yet. The question that arrives right now — the one you answer before the moment passes — stays. For everyone in your network. For the ones who come after.

Your Eternal is what it was, and what it will be.
Your Current is this moment, moving through you into someone else's.
Both belong only to you. Forever.
