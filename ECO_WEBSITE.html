How it works

Built around how families actually work — not how software thinks they should

No forms. No trees. No one at the top. ECO is a conversation between you and your family's story — guided by an AI that asks open questions and never assumes what you believe, practice, or remember.

01
The AI Conversation
It asks. You tell. It listens.
There are no forms on ECO. Not a single one. Every piece of information enters through conversation — an AI that asks one question at a time, at the pace of memory, and never pushes harder than you want to go.

The AI never assumes. It will not tell you that you will "tie the chudda at Sahiba's wedding" before knowing whether you were invited. It asks: "Sahiba's wedding is coming up — which activities will you have a chance to participate in?" Your answer teaches it. Then it goes deeper.
AI Question · Milestone · Upcoming
"When Sahiba — or maybe one of her parents, or her sister — added her to the network, her upcoming wedding sounds like a fun chance for the whole family. As her mama, which activities will you have a chance to participate in? Is there anything you're especially excited about?"

The manifesto
We built this because
nothing else
held our story.
I
You are not a leaf on a tree

Every genealogy platform ever built puts someone at the top. A founding ancestor. A patriarch. A root from which everyone else grows as a leaf.

This is not how families work. This is how one culture decided to represent them — and then exported that model to the entire world.

A Punjabi family spread across India, Fiji, the United Kingdom, Canada, and the United States is not a tree. It is a field of energy — a network of connections radiating outward from every person at its center simultaneously.

ECO puts you at the center. Not your great-great-grandfather. Not whoever founded the lineage. You. The person using the platform. The person whose story is still being written.

II
The words that disappear

In North Indian families, the word for a maternal uncle is Mama. The word for a maternal grandfather is Nana. In English, Nana often means grandmother. The same sound. Completely different people.

A diaspora child who grows up speaking English may call their grandmother Nana — because that is what English tells them to do — and unknowingly erase the word that their grandparents used for a completely different relationship.

This is not failure. It is what happens when a language does not have the right words for your family. It is what ECO was built to resist.

The word Bhayia, in Hindi and Punjabi, means older brother. But in one family we know, a son calls his father Bhayia — because he grew up watching his aunt and uncle use the word, and it jumped a generation through observation and love rather than formal teaching.

That is a documented fact about a real family. It lives in ECO because ECO was built to hold it. No other platform in the world makes space for that sentence.

III
We never assume what you practice

A North Indian name, a family from Amritsar, a network full of Punjabi kinship terms — none of this tells ECO what you believe, practice, observe, or identify with.

Roots are not practice. Heritage is not identity. Geography is not religion.

ECO's AI will never tell you what your tradition requires of you. It will ask what you are planning to do, and listen to your answer.

This is not a hedge. It is not legal caution. It is the philosophy of the platform: you are the authority on your own family. ECO holds what you tell it. It asks about everything else.

If the AI asks a question that assumes something wrong, you can correct it. The correction teaches the platform. The platform becomes more accurate for everyone in a network like yours. The data improves because you participate, not because ECO presumes.

IV
Your data is not a product

In March 2025, 23andMe filed for bankruptcy. The genetic data of 15 million people — including their ancestry, their health information, their family connections — was put up for auction.

The people who provided that data had agreed to terms of service. The terms of service had contemplated this possibility. None of them expected it to happen.

"Only Yours. Forever." is not a marketing phrase. It is a structural commitment built into every architectural decision ECO has made.

ECO does not sell data. ECO does not monetize behavioral patterns. ECO does not carry advertising. ECO does not share your family's information with pharmaceutical companies, insurers, governments, or anyone else.

The subscription is the revenue. The data is yours. These two sentences are the entire business model.

V
Before it is too late

There is a specific kind of urgency that ECO was built around. It is not the urgency of a product launch or a market window. It is the urgency of biology.

The people who hold the most irreplaceable knowledge — the stories of displacement, the reason a family left a city, the name of the street where your grandmother grew up, the word your great-grandfather used for the relationship between a man and his wife's brothers — are not getting younger.

When they are gone, the window closes. No amount of technology, no database, no AI can recover what was never recorded.

ECO's AI question system is weighted 70–80% toward historical and passed figures. Toward deep history. Toward the people your Conductors never met — grandparents who passed early, relatives separated by Partition, ancestors whose stories exist only in the fading memory of the generation above you.

The platform asks about the living because they need to know their family. It asks about the dead because it is almost too late to ask at all.

VI
What we commit to
No forms. Not a single HTML form tag anywhere on the platform. Every data input is a conversation, a tap, a search, or a file upload. Data entry should feel like memory, not paperwork.
No trees. The constellation and the globe are the visualization models. Hierarchy belongs to people, not to software defaults.
No assumptions. The AI never tells you what your tradition requires, who has which role, or what your heritage means to you. It asks. It listens. It learns from your answer.
No advertising. ECO is a space to think, not a surface to sell. The subscription is the business model. Your attention is not the product.
No deletion without your request. If you stop paying, your network is frozen — intact, preserved, exactly as you left it — for twelve months. Your family's story does not disappear because a subscription lapsed.
No data sales. Ever. Not now. Not if we grow. Not if we are acquired. Not if the business faces difficulty. The commitment survives ownership changes because it is built into the architecture, not just the policy.
Complete removal, always. Ask for erasure. It happens within 30 days. No penalty. No questions. Your data is gone.
