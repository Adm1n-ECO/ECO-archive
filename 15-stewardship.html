15 — Stewardship & Memory
The memory lifecycle

Every branch stays alive.

Profiles don't disappear when people go quiet or pass on. The tree remembers. Others can step forward to keep the memory present — paying forward the love that was given to them.

The headline no other product can use "Some branches go quiet. Not forever — only until someone who remembers them steps forward." This is ECO's deepest differentiator. It belongs on the homepage, not in a feature list. Flag for Isaiah.

Adopting a shadow profile
1
See the shadow on a connected tree
A silhouette labelled "Grandmother · Lahore" appears on a tree you're connected to. You recognise who it is — your great-grandmother. No one has claimed her yet.
2
Tap "Keep this memory alive"
A screen appears confirming the relationship, city, and the current state. Anyone on ECO can adopt — no family connection required. First to pay activates it.
3
Pay $1.99 — profile activates
The profile transitions from shadow to "kept alive" state. The name appears. You become the lead steward. Others can now join as co-stewards.
4
Add photos, stories, history
Up to 3 photos + 3 videos on the standard profile. Upgrade to unlimited memory for $5.99/month per profile. Other stewards can contribute simultaneously.
✦
Optional — mark as In Memoriam
If the person has passed, any steward or direct connection can mark the profile as In Memoriam — with a confirmation window so others can acknowledge before it's finalised.
✓ Open stewardship — any ECO member, first to pay activates it. Creates a viral discovery mechanic where people search shadow profiles on connected trees looking for relatives they've lost touch with.

Date format — privacy model
Birth and passing dates are collected and stored as month + year only.
"Born March 1924" — not "Born 14 March 1924".
This protects against identity reconstruction while preserving the meaningful temporal context for family.
Decision 28 still open — who can mark as In Memoriam? Options: (A) any steward or direct connection, 7-day dispute window, (B) requires 2 family confirmations, (C) lead steward only, (D) open to any ECO member. Awaiting answer.

The privacy principle — say it explicitly in the product "We collect what connects people, not what exposes them." Month and year instead of exact date. City instead of address. Relationship instead of name (for shadows). This isn't just a security decision — it's a philosophical statement about what this product values. It belongs in the Privacy Policy, the About page, and the onboarding flow. For the families this product serves — multigenerational, globally dispersed, from regions where data privacy has real stakes — this framing will build more trust than any security certification.
